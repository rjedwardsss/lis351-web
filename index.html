Featured build roadmap

Most builds go wrong for one reason: no order. Start with maintenance and basics, then handling, then power. This keeps the car safe, fun, and consistent while you upgrade.

Want a deeper breakdown? Read the starter mod checklist and the what not to do section.

Starter mod checklist

If you’re new to modding, these upgrades are usually the safest way to improve the car without ruining reliability.

  • Tires that match your driving goals (daily, performance, all-season)
  • Brake pads/rotors if needed, plus fresh brake fluid
  • Alignment after suspension or wheel changes
  • Tint and lighting upgrades for a cleaner look
  • Basic interior changes (shift knob, floor mats, clean detailing)

What not to do

You can save yourself a lot of pain by avoiding “random mods first.” These usually cost more in the long run.

  • Buying the cheapest parts just because they’re on sale
  • Doing power mods before maintenance and handling upgrades
  • Ignoring safety, worn tires, or braking issues
  • Installing parts that don’t fit your actual goals for the car
